Step 1 — Propose the Charter Structure
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Before writing CHAR-001, propose its table of contents.

For each section explain:

• Why it exists.
• Why it belongs in the Charter instead of another document.
• What information belongs in the section.
• What information must NOT be placed there.

The Charter should remain intentionally short.

Target length:

2–4 pages.

If a section would cause the Charter to grow unnecessarily, recommend removing it.

Mandatory Sections
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

At minimum evaluate these sections:

1. Mission
2. North Star
3. Product Philosophy
4. Core Principles
5. Project Values
6. Explicit Non-Goals

You may recommend one or two additional sections only if they provide clear long-term value.

Avoid adding sections simply because they are common in enterprise documentation.

Additional Design Questions
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Please also answer the following:

1. What tone should the Charter have?

Examples:

• Visionary
• Technical
• Product-oriented
• Formal
• Lightweight

Recommend one style and explain why.

2. Should the Charter contain diagrams?

If yes, what type?

If not, explain why.

3. Should the Charter reference other documents?

Or should it remain completely standalone?

Explain the trade-offs.

4. Which future documents should reference the Charter?

List them.
